Cross-Federation Trust via OpenID Federation Trust Marks

Abstract

OpenID Federation 1.1 establishes trust between two Entities by resolving a Trust Chain to a common Trust Anchor. This requirement prevents a Relying Party in one federation from trusting an Entity whose Entity Statements are anchored only in a different federation, unless a common superior Trust Anchor or out-of-band cross-configuration exists.¶

This profile defines how a Trust Mark issued within an Accrediting Federation can carry the information needed for a Relying Party in that federation to establish trust in a Credential Issuer anchored in a separate Credential Issuer Federation, without a shared Trust Anchor and without pre-configuring the Credential Issuer Federation's Trust Anchor. It further defines how such a Trust Mark is bound to the Verifiable Credentials issued by that Credential Issuer using the W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model.¶

1. Introduction

In a typical OpenID Federation deployment a Relying Party trusts one or more Trust Anchors that are configured out of band. An Entity is trusted when a valid Trust Chain can be resolved from that Entity to one of those configured Trust Anchors ([OpenID.Federation], Section 10). Trust Marks (Section 7) are evaluated separately: a Trust Mark is a signed JWT asserting that its subject conforms to a set of criteria defined by an accreditation authority, and a Relying Party trusts a Trust Mark when the Trust Mark Issuer is recognized for that Trust Mark type by a Trust Anchor the Relying Party uses.¶

This profile addresses the case in which the accreditation and the accredited Entity live in different federations:¶

  • The Credential Issuer is a Leaf Entity whose Entity Statements are anchored in the Credential Issuer Federation.¶

  • The accreditation is expressed as a Trust Mark issued by a Trust Mark Issuer recognized in the Accrediting Federation.¶

  • A Relying Party that trusts the Accrediting Federation's Trust Anchor needs to trust the accreditation and to verify the authenticity of the Credential Issuer and its issued Credentials, even though it does not natively trust the Credential Issuer Federation's Trust Anchor.¶

The mechanism is to convey, inside the Trust Mark, the identity and keys of the Credential Issuer Federation's Trust Anchor, so the Relying Party can resolve and validate the Credential Issuer's Trust Chain on the basis of a Trust Mark it already trusts.¶

1.1. Requirements Notation and Conventions

The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "NOT RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as described in BCP 14 [RFC2119] [RFC8174] when, and only when, they appear in all capitals, as shown here.¶

This profile uses the terms defined in [OpenID.Federation]. Unless otherwise stated, all base-specification processing rules continue to apply; this profile only adds requirements.¶

1.2. Terminology

This profile defines the following terms in addition to those of [OpenID.Federation].¶

Accrediting Federation (AF):
The federation whose Trust Anchor recognizes the Trust Mark Issuer for the Trust Mark type defined by a deployment of this profile, via the trust_mark_issuers Claim.¶
Credential Issuer Federation (CIF):
The federation in which the Credential Issuer is a Leaf Entity and whose Trust Anchor is authoritative for that Credential Issuer's Entity Statements.¶
Credential Issuer Trust Anchor:
The Trust Anchor of the Credential Issuer Federation, at which the Credential Issuer's Trust Chain terminates.¶
Credential Issuer (CI):
The Entity that issues Verifiable Credentials and that is the sub of the Trust Mark. The Credential Issuer is a Leaf Entity in the Credential Issuer Federation.¶
Trust Mark Issuer (TMI):
The accreditation authority that issues the Trust Mark, recognized in the Accrediting Federation. Defined in [OpenID.Federation].¶
Relying Party (RP):
The party consuming the Verifiable Credential (the Verifier), which trusts the Accrediting Federation's Trust Anchor.¶

The Accrediting Federation and the Credential Issuer Federation MAY be operated by different organizations and MUST NOT be assumed to share a Trust Anchor.¶

1.3. Relationship to OpenID Federation 1.1

This document is a profile (extension) of [OpenID.Federation]. It defines additional Claims that appear within Trust Mark JWTs (Section 7.1 of the base specification permits additional members), additional Relying Party processing rules, and a binding of Trust Marks to Verifiable Credentials. It introduces no changes to Entity Statement, Trust Chain, or Federation Endpoint processing.¶

2. Architecture

An Entity acting under this profile participates in two distinct and orthogonal trust relationships:¶

  1. Accreditation recognition (Accrediting Federation). The Relying Party trusts the Trust Mark because the Trust Mark Issuer is recognized by the AF Trust Anchor for the relevant Trust Mark type. This is the standard Trust Mark trust model of [OpenID.Federation], Section 7.¶

  2. Credential Issuer authenticity (Credential Issuer Federation). The Relying Party establishes the Credential Issuer's keys and metadata by resolving a Trust Chain that terminates at the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor. The Relying Party learns to trust the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor from the Trust Mark, which pins that Trust Anchor's identity and keys.¶

These two relationships meet only at the Relying Party. The Trust Mark Issuer's own Trust Chain MUST resolve to a Trust Anchor the Relying Party trusts (the AF Trust Anchor); the Credential Issuer's Trust Chain resolves to the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor pinned in the Trust Mark.¶

The recognition of the Trust Mark Issuer cannot itself be bootstrapped from the Trust Mark, because that recognition is the root of the Relying Party's trust in the accreditation. Consequently, this profile pins only the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor inside the Trust Mark; the Trust Mark Issuer MUST be recognized natively in the Accrediting Federation (Section 8.2).¶

3. Trust Mark Type

A deployment of this profile MUST define one Trust Mark type identifier, owned by the Accrediting Federation. The Trust Mark type identifier is a URL, collision-resistant across federations, and SHOULD be constructed from a URL that uniquely identifies the Accrediting Federation or trust framework.¶

The Trust Mark Definition referenced by the Trust Mark ref Claim MUST specify, at minimum: the accreditation criteria; the set of credential types in scope; any additional matching constraints (Section 4.3); the renewal and revocation process; and the Trust Chain Constraints ([OpenID.Federation], Section 6.2) that a Relying Party MUST apply to the Credential Issuer's Trust Chain (Section 7, step 6).¶

4. Trust Mark Profile

A Trust Mark conforming to this profile is a JWT with the typ header parameter trust-mark+jwt and the Claims defined in [OpenID.Federation], Section 7.1 (iss, sub, trust_mark_type, iat, and optionally exp, ref, delegation, logo_uri). In addition, the following Claims are defined by this profile.¶

4.1. credential_issuer_trust_anchor

REQUIRED. String. The Entity Identifier of the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor, i.e. the Trust Anchor at which the Trust Chain for the Entity identified by the sub Claim MUST terminate. The value MUST be an Entity Identifier as defined by [OpenID.Federation].¶

4.2. credential_issuer_trust_anchor_jwks_thumbprints

REQUIRED. Array of strings. A non-empty array of JWK SHA-256 Thumbprints [RFC7638], base64url-encoded, each identifying a Federation Entity Key of the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor identified by credential_issuer_trust_anchor.¶

When validating the Credential Issuer's Trust Chain, the Relying Party MUST verify that the terminating Trust Anchor publishes at least one Federation Entity Key whose thumbprint matches a value in this array, and MUST use only such matched key(s) as the trust root for that Trust Chain.¶

Thumbprints, rather than a full JWK Set, are pinned so that Credential Issuer Trust Anchor key rollover does not invalidate outstanding Trust Marks; multiple values MAY be present to span a rollover. A deployment that requires re-accreditation on Credential Issuer Trust Anchor key rollover MAY instead pin a single thumbprint.¶

Note: pinning the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor's keys (rather than only its Entity Identifier) ensures the security of the cross-federation Trust Chain reduces to the Trust Mark Issuer's signature, and does not depend on transport security at the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor's endpoints.¶

4.3. accredited_credentials

REQUIRED. A non-empty JSON array of objects. Each object identifies a credential type that the Credential Issuer (sub) is accredited to issue under this Trust Mark, and MAY further constrain the accreditation. Each object has the following members:¶

format:
REQUIRED. String. The Credential Format Identifier (for example, a format identifier registered for OpenID for Verifiable Credential Issuance), such as ldp_vc, jwt_vc_json, or dc+sd-jwt.¶
(type identifier):
A credential type identifier appropriate to format. For W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model formats (e.g. ldp_vc, jwt_vc_json), type is REQUIRED and is a JSON array of strings naming the Credential type values, other than the base VerifiableCredential, that scope the accreditation. For IETF SD-JWT VC (dc+sd-jwt), vct is REQUIRED and is the string Verifiable Credential Type.¶
constraints:
OPTIONAL. A JSON object of additional Claim constraints that a presented Verifiable Credential MUST satisfy to be considered covered by this accreditation entry (for example, a qualification identifier carried as a Credential Claim). The interpretation of constraints is defined by the Trust Mark Definition (Section 3).¶

A deployment SHOULD issue one Trust Mark per (Credential Issuer, credential type) pair, so that the Trust Mark Status mechanism ([OpenID.Federation], Section 8.4) can revoke a single accreditation independently of others.¶

The credential type identifiers in accredited_credentials are governed by their respective type authorities and are independent of both the Accrediting Federation and the Credential Issuer Federation.¶

5. Trust Mark Delegation

Where the Trust Mark Owner and the Trust Mark Issuer are different Entities - which is the expected case for this profile, with the Accrediting Federation operator owning the Trust Mark type and an accreditation authority issuing it - the Trust Mark MUST contain a delegation Claim as defined in [OpenID.Federation], Section 7.2, and the AF Trust Anchor MUST publish the corresponding trust_mark_owners entry (Section 6).¶

6. Accrediting Federation Trust Anchor Configuration

The Accrediting Federation Trust Anchor MUST recognize the Trust Mark Issuer for the profile's Trust Mark type by listing it in the trust_mark_issuers Claim of its Entity Configuration.¶

Where Trust Mark Delegation is used (Section 5), the AF Trust Anchor MUST publish a trust_mark_owners entry for the Trust Mark type, containing the Owner's Entity Identifier (sub) and the Owner's signing keys (jwks).¶

7. Relying Party Processing

On receiving a Verifiable Credential whose acceptance is gated on accreditation under this profile, the Relying Party MUST perform all of the following steps. Failure of any step means the accreditation is not established and the Credential MUST NOT be accepted on the basis of this profile.¶

  1. Obtain the Trust Mark. Obtain the Trust Mark from one of: the Credential Issuer's trust_marks Claim (Section 8); the Trust Mark Issuer's Trust Marked Entities Listing ([OpenID.Federation], Section 8.5); or the Verifiable Credential itself (Section 9). The trust_mark_type MUST equal the Trust Mark type expected by the Relying Party.¶

  2. Recognize the Trust Mark Issuer. The Trust Mark iss MUST appear in the Relying Party's Accrediting Federation Trust Anchor trust_mark_issuers Claim for this trust_mark_type. The Relying Party MUST resolve and validate a Trust Chain for the Trust Mark Issuer to that Trust Anchor ([OpenID.Federation], Sections 7.3 and 10) and obtain the Issuer's Federation Entity Keys.¶

  3. Validate delegation. If the Trust Mark contains a delegation Claim, the Relying Party MUST validate it against the trust_mark_owners entry for this trust_mark_type in the AF Trust Anchor, per [OpenID.Federation], Section 7.2.¶

  4. Verify the Trust Mark. The Relying Party MUST verify the Trust Mark signature using the Trust Mark Issuer's Federation Entity Keys, and MUST verify iat and, if present, exp.¶

  5. Bind subject to issuer. The Trust Mark sub MUST equal the iss (issuer identifier) of the Verifiable Credential.¶

  6. Resolve the Credential Issuer Trust Chain. The Relying Party MUST resolve a Trust Chain for the Verifiable Credential's issuer that terminates at credential_issuer_trust_anchor. The terminating Trust Anchor MUST publish a Federation Entity Key whose JWK SHA-256 Thumbprint matches a value in credential_issuer_trust_anchor_jwks_thumbprints, and that key MUST be used as the trust root. The Relying Party MUST apply the Trust Chain Constraints specified by the Trust Mark Definition (Section 3) to this Trust Chain.¶

  7. Verify Credential authenticity. The Relying Party MUST verify the Verifiable Credential using the issuer keys established by the resolved Trust Chain.¶

  8. Verify coverage. The Verifiable Credential MUST match at least one entry of accredited_credentials: its format and credential type identifier MUST match, and any constraints in that entry MUST be satisfied by the Credential.¶

  9. Verify status. Unless the Relying Party's policy permits reliance on the Trust Mark exp alone, the Relying Party MUST verify that the Trust Mark is active using the Trust Mark Status mechanism ([OpenID.Federation], Section 8.4).¶

A Relying Party that already trusts credential_issuer_trust_anchor natively MAY omit the thumbprint comparison in step 6, but MUST still require the Trust Chain to terminate at credential_issuer_trust_anchor.¶

8. Advertisement and Trust Domains

8.1. Advertisement by the Credential Issuer

The Credential Issuer SHOULD advertise the Trust Mark in the trust_marks Claim of its Entity Configuration, so that Relying Parties can discover it during normal resolution. Each array entry contains the trust_mark_type and the compact serialization of the trust-mark+jwt ([OpenID.Federation], Section 3).¶

The Trust Mark is signed by the Trust Mark Issuer. Its inclusion in the Credential Issuer's self-signed Entity Configuration is solely a means of advertisement and carries no trust weight of its own; the Credential Issuer's signature over its Entity Configuration does not attest to the Trust Mark's content. The Trust Mark's authority derives entirely from the Trust Mark Issuer's signature, validated per Section 7.¶

8.2. Trust Domains

Recognition of the Trust Mark Issuer is REQUIRED only within the federation(s) whose Relying Parties act on the Trust Mark - that is, the Accrediting Federation. The Credential Issuer Federation is NOT REQUIRED to recognize the Trust Mark Issuer, and its Trust Anchor neither validates nor endorses Trust Marks carried in its subordinate Entities' Entity Configurations. An unrecognized Trust Mark is ignored by parties that do not recognize its Issuer and does not affect the validity of the Credential Issuer's Entity Statements or Trust Chain within the Credential Issuer Federation.¶

8.3. Resolver Behaviour

A Resolver ([OpenID.Federation], Section 8.3) returns only verified Trust Marks and omits Trust Marks it cannot verify or that are not active. A Resolver operating in the Credential Issuer Federation therefore omits Accrediting-Federation Trust Marks from its resolve responses, because it does not recognize the Trust Mark Issuer; this is expected and is not an error.¶

Consequently, a Relying Party MUST validate the Trust Mark within a trust context in which the Trust Mark Issuer is recognized (that is, against the Accrediting Federation Trust Anchor, per Section 7). A Relying Party MUST NOT rely on a Credential-Issuer-Federation resolve response having included the Trust Mark in its verified set.¶

9. Binding the Trust Mark to a Verifiable Credential

A Credential Issuer MAY embed the Trust Mark in the Verifiable Credentials it issues. Embedding is an OPTIONAL convenience that supports offline and single-message verification; it does not replace the authoritative source (the Credential Issuer's trust_marks Claim and the Trust Mark Status mechanism). Where current accreditation status is required by policy, the Relying Party MUST still perform Section 7, step 9.¶

9.1. W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model

For credentials expressed using the W3C Verifiable Credentials Data Model 2.0 [VCDM2], the Trust Mark MUST be conveyed using the termsOfUse property, which carries issuer-asserted policy under which the Credential was issued. This profile defines a termsOfUse entry with the following shape:¶

type:
REQUIRED. MUST include the value OpenIDFederationTrustMark.¶
trust_mark_type:
REQUIRED. String. MUST equal the embedded Trust Mark's trust_mark_type.¶
trust_mark:
REQUIRED. String. The compact serialization of the trust-mark+jwt.¶

The type value and its properties MUST be defined by a JSON-LD context that is referenced from the Credential's @context. A Relying Party processing the embedded Trust Mark MUST validate it per Section 7; the embedded representation is informative until so validated.¶

Example (non-normative):¶

"termsOfUse": [
  {
    "type": "OpenIDFederationTrustMark",
    "trust_mark_type": "https://accrediting.example/trust-marks/accredited-issuer",
    "trust_mark": "<compact trust-mark+jwt>"
  }
]
¶

The Trust Mark exp, if present, SHOULD NOT be later than the Credential's own expiry. A Credential Issuer MUST define a policy for Credentials already issued at the time an accreditation is subsequently revoked.¶

9.2. Other Credential Formats

For Credential formats that do not support termsOfUse (for example, IETF SD-JWT VC [SD-JWT-VC]), a deployment MAY define an equivalent claim carrying the same three members. Such a claim SHOULD be present unconditionally (not selectively disclosable), since it concerns the issuer rather than the subject and a Relying Party requires it to evaluate accreditation. The processing rules of Section 7 apply unchanged.¶

10. Security Considerations

The security considerations of [OpenID.Federation] apply. In addition:¶

  • The Trust Mark Issuer is a cross-federation trust-anchor authority. Trusting a Trust Mark means trusting the credential_issuer_trust_anchor and credential_issuer_trust_anchor_jwks_thumbprints it asserts. A compromised or negligent Trust Mark Issuer can introduce an attacker-controlled Trust Anchor under which forged Credentials resolve. Deployments SHOULD constrain the Trust Mark Issuer's authority - for example, the Accrediting Federation Trust Anchor MAY publish an allow-list of permitted Credential Issuer Trust Anchors or Credential Issuer Federations, and Relying Parties MAY require credential_issuer_trust_anchor to appear on that allow-list.¶

  • Identifier binding. A Relying Party MUST bind, in a single evaluation, that the Trust Mark sub, the Verifiable Credential issuer identifier, and the subject of the resolved Trust Chain are the same Entity, and that this Trust Chain terminates at the pinned credential_issuer_trust_anchor. Omitting any leg permits substitution attacks.¶

  • Key pinning. Pinning Credential Issuer Trust Anchor keys (Section 4.2), rather than only its identifier, prevents the cross-federation bootstrap from depending on transport-layer security at the Credential Issuer Trust Anchor's endpoints.¶

  • Constraints on dynamically introduced Trust Anchors. Deployment-side per-Trust-Anchor constraints (entity type, naming, maximum path length) cannot be applied to a Credential Issuer Trust Anchor that is not configured at the Relying Party. Such constraints MUST instead be expressed in the Trust Mark Definition (Section 3) and applied per Section 7, step 6, so that all Relying Parties scope Credential-Issuer-Federation Trust Chains identically.¶

  • Non-bootstrappable Issuer. The Trust Mark Issuer MUST be recognized and resolvable in the Accrediting Federation independently of the Trust Mark; a deployment MUST NOT attempt to convey the Trust Mark Issuer's trust root inside the Trust Mark.¶

11. Privacy Considerations

The privacy considerations of [OpenID.Federation] apply, including those for the Trust Mark Status mechanism (Section 19.2), which can disclose a Relying Party's interest in a particular subject to the Trust Mark Issuer.¶

A Trust Mark under this profile asserts facts about the Credential Issuer, not about the Credential subject, and contains no subject personal data; embedding it in a Credential (Section 9) introduces no additional correlation of the subject.¶

12. IANA Considerations

This profile defines the Trust Mark Claims credential_issuer_trust_anchor, credential_issuer_trust_anchor_jwks_thumbprints, and accredited_credentials for use within trust-mark+jwt JWTs, and the W3C VCDM termsOfUse type OpenIDFederationTrustMark. Registration of these names in the appropriate registries is to be determined.¶
